About Sign Language

Sign language is a visual language that uses hand shapes, facial expressions, gestures, and body language to communicate. It is the primary language of many deaf people around the world, with different countries and regions having their own sign languages.

American Sign Language (ASL) is used in the United States and parts of Canada. It is a complete, complex language with its own grammar and syntax that differs from English. British Sign Language (BSL) is used in the United Kingdom and is different from ASL.

Sign language is not universal, and there are over 300 different sign languages worldwide. Each sign language has its own vocabulary and grammatical structure. Many deaf communities consider their sign language an important part of their cultural identity.

Learning sign language can enhance communication between deaf and hearing individuals, breaking down barriers and fostering inclusion. Our SignAI tool aims to bridge this gap using technology to interpret and translate sign language in real-time.
